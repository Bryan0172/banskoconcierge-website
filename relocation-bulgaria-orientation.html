What most people underestimate

Moving to Bulgaria is easier when you understand the local reality.

Bulgaria attracts a growing number of international residents — remote workers, retirees, property buyers, families and individuals looking for a different cost of living and quality of life. The country is genuinely accessible: EU membership, relatively low costs, good climate and a straightforward path for EU nationals to establish residence.

What most people underestimate is the gap between what Bulgaria looks like from the outside and how it actually works on the ground. Healthcare navigation, local banking, property markets, regional differences between Bansko, Sofia and the coast — these are not difficult questions, but they require local knowledge that takes time to build.

Relocation orientation provides direct access to that knowledge before or shortly after you arrive, so you start with a realistic picture instead of assumptions.

Practical logistics

Bringing your car to Bulgaria

If you are relocating with a vehicle, re-registration in Bulgaria is usually required once you establish local residence. The process involves a technical inspection, document preparation, a NOx environmental charge, and registration at KAT. For non-EU vehicles, customs clearance applies first.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

What is Relocation Bulgaria Orientation?
A practical local briefing for individuals and families planning to move to or settle in Bulgaria. It covers location choice, everyday setup, healthcare navigation, schooling options, property market realities and access to trusted local contacts. It is not a formal immigration or legal service.
Do EU citizens need a visa to live in Bulgaria?
EU citizens can live and work in Bulgaria without a visa. For stays beyond three months, registration with local authorities is required. Relocation Orientation covers what that process involves in practice, but formal residency applications require a licensed immigration professional.
What are the practical differences between living in Bansko, Sofia and the Bulgarian coast?
Each location has a distinct character. Bansko is a mountain resort town with a strong international community, lower cost of living and proximity to ski and outdoor activities. Sofia is the capital, with full urban infrastructure and the widest range of services. The coast has seasonal character with a more relaxed pace. Orientation covers which option suits your situation honestly.
Can Bansko Concierge help foreign nationals open a bank account in Bulgaria?
We can provide guidance on what to expect and which options are generally accessible to foreign nationals, but bank account opening is subject to individual bank decisions and we cannot guarantee any specific outcome. We will point you to the options most commonly used by the international community.
Is Bulgaria a good choice for remote workers relocating from Western Europe?
Bulgaria offers a combination of EU membership, relatively low costs, fast internet connectivity and a growing international community in key locations. Relocation Orientation provides a direct, honest local picture — including the practical limitations that are not visible from online research.
What does Relocation Bulgaria Orientation not cover?
It does not provide immigration advice, legal representation, tax planning, official residency application support, certified translation or notarial services. Where these are needed, we will point you to the right local professionals.
How do I arrange a relocation orientation conversation with Bansko Concierge?
Contact us via WhatsApp or email with a brief description of your situation and timeline. We will confirm what we can cover and schedule a conversation based on your availability.
Do I need to register my car in Bulgaria after moving?
If you establish residence in Bulgaria, your vehicle will typically need to be re-registered with Bulgarian authorities. The process involves a technical inspection, document preparation, a NOx environmental tax, and registration at KAT. For non-EU vehicles, customs clearance applies first. See our Car Import and Registration Bulgaria page for a full overview of what the process involves and how Bansko Concierge can coordinate the practical steps.
