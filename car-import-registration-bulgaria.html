What changes when you move

Bringing a car to Bulgaria is more involved than a border crossing.

Driving into Bulgaria on a foreign plate for a holiday or short visit is straightforward. Moving to Bulgaria and bringing your vehicle with you is a different matter. Once you establish residency, Bulgarian rules apply to your car — and the process involves several overlapping steps: customs clearance for non-EU vehicles, local registration, insurance requirements, roadworthiness testing and, in some cases, a change of driving licence.

The sequence matters, the documentation requirements are specific, and the timelines depend on your situation. This page gives you a practical overview of what is typically involved — but it does not replace the advice of a local lawyer or specialist who can assess your specific vehicle, registration country and residency status.

Important note

This page provides general orientation only. It is not legal, customs or tax advice. Requirements change and vary by individual situation. Always confirm current rules with a qualified Bulgarian legal or registration specialist before acting.

Vehicle registration overview

What car registration in Bulgaria typically involves

For EU-registered vehicles brought by a relocating EU resident, the process is typically more straightforward than for cars imported from outside the EU. Key steps that most people navigate include:

  • Technical inspection (GOT) — a roadworthiness check at an authorised Bulgarian testing station
  • Document assembly — original registration certificate, proof of ownership, valid identity documents, insurance
  • Emissions assessment — Bulgaria applies an environmental (NOx/CO2) tax on certain vehicle types at the point of registration; the amount depends on the specific vehicle
  • Customs clearance — required for vehicles arriving from outside the EU, handled by a licensed customs agent
  • Registration at the local KAT office — the Road Police and Administrative Service, which issues Bulgarian plates
  • Bulgarian insurance — a Bulgarian-issued third-party liability policy is required for a Bulgarian-registered vehicle

The order of these steps, the documents required at each stage and the timeframes involved depend on your specific situation. A local specialist can walk through the sequence with you and identify what needs to happen first.

Driving licence exchange

Do you need to exchange your driving licence?

For EU and EEA licence holders, Bulgarian regulations generally allow continued use of your existing licence without exchange, even after establishing residency. There are exceptions — particularly for licences that are approaching expiry or were issued in countries with specific bilateral arrangements.

For non-EU licence holders, an exchange process typically applies after establishing residency in Bulgaria. The process, applicable timeframe and any required testing vary by the country of issue. Some nationalities face a straightforward administrative exchange; others may be required to pass a Bulgarian driving test.

The rules in this area are updated periodically. We recommend confirming the current requirements with a local lawyer or the relevant Bulgarian authority before assuming your licence situation is straightforward — particularly if you are relocating permanently or for an extended period.

This is not legal advice

Driving licence exchange rules are set by Bulgarian law and depend on your specific nationality and residency status. We cannot guarantee outcomes or timelines. Confirm the current requirements with a qualified local adviser.

Car insurance in Bulgaria

What you need to know about Bulgarian motor insurance

Bulgaria is a member of the Green Card scheme, which means EU-issued motor insurance policies typically provide at least minimum third-party coverage for travel in Bulgaria. For short visits, your existing insurance is likely sufficient — confirm with your insurer.

Once your vehicle is registered in Bulgaria, a Bulgarian-issued third-party liability (GO) policy becomes mandatory. This is straightforward to arrange through Bulgarian insurers and is widely available. The cost depends on the vehicle type, age and your personal profile.

For a vehicle that is registered abroad but used long-term in Bulgaria, the insurance situation is more nuanced — your insurer should confirm in writing what is covered and for how long, particularly if you are near the threshold of establishing residency.

Flexible situations

Non-resident use, short-term stays and vehicle storage

Not everyone who brings a car to Bulgaria is relocating permanently. Some international property owners drive their vehicle to Bulgaria for the season and leave it there. Others use a foreign-registered vehicle for regular short stays without establishing residency. Each of these situations has different implications.

  • Seasonal use — a foreign-registered car can typically remain in Bulgaria for a defined period without requiring re-registration, subject to the vehicle's home-country rules and your residency status
  • Long-term storage — if you leave a car in Bulgaria for months at a time, it is worth confirming with your insurer and a local adviser what your obligations are regarding insurance continuity and registration status
  • Vehicle transport — if you want to ship or transport your car to Bulgaria without driving it yourself, we can help coordinate the local receiving logistics
  • Selling a foreign-registered car in Bulgaria — this involves specific import and registration steps and should be done with a local specialist

If your situation is unusual or you are unsure which category applies to you, the right starting point is a conversation with a local lawyer who knows the current rules — we can connect you with one.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to register my foreign car in Bulgaria if I move there?
If you establish residency in Bulgaria, Bulgarian registration rules generally apply to your vehicle after a defined period. The specific requirements depend on where the car is currently registered, your residency status and the vehicle type. We recommend confirming the current rules with a local lawyer or the relevant Bulgarian authority, as procedures and timelines can change. What we can help with is the practical local coordination once you know what is required.
Can Bansko Concierge handle the car registration process for me?
We are not a customs agent, legal representative or licensed vehicle registration service. What we can do is help coordinate the practical logistics: connecting you with a trusted local contact who handles vehicle registration paperwork, accompanying you to appointments if needed, helping with translation and making sure you have the right documents assembled before you go. The official process requires your direct involvement or a formally authorised representative.
What documents are typically needed to register a foreign car in Bulgaria?
The typical document set includes the original vehicle registration certificate, proof of ownership, valid identity documents, valid insurance, a customs clearance certificate (for vehicles from outside the EU), and any relevant roadworthiness inspection results. Requirements can vary and change — confirm the current list with a local specialist before travel to avoid delays.
Do I need to exchange my foreign driving licence when I move to Bulgaria?
EU and EEA licence holders generally do not need to exchange their licence when living in Bulgaria. Non-EU licence holders typically face exchange requirements after establishing residency, subject to deadlines set by Bulgarian regulations. The process and timeline vary by country of issue. We can connect you with the right local contact — but specific legal requirements should be confirmed with a qualified adviser.
Is car insurance from my home country valid in Bulgaria?
Bulgaria is a member of the Green Card insurance scheme, so most EU-issued motor insurance policies include at least minimum coverage for travel in Bulgaria. For longer stays or permanent residence, a Bulgarian motor insurance policy is typically required. Your insurer can confirm what your current policy covers and when a separate Bulgarian policy becomes necessary.
What is the NOx tax and does it apply to my vehicle?
Bulgaria applies an environmental tax on certain vehicle registrations based on emissions. Whether and how much applies to your vehicle depends on the engine type, emission standard and whether the car was previously registered in an EU country. A local registration specialist can assess this for your specific vehicle before you commit to the process.
Can I drive my foreign-registered car in Bulgaria without registering it?
Visitors and short-term stays can generally use a foreign-registered vehicle in Bulgaria. Once you establish residency, registration requirements apply. The point at which this becomes obligatory depends on your residency status — we recommend clarifying this with a legal adviser before your move to avoid fines or complications at border crossings.
