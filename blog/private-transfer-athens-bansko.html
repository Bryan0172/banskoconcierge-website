How to Get from Athens Airport to Bansko: All Transfer Options Compared [2026]

AD
Andreas Donner
· 27 March 2026 · 6 min read · Transfers

The Athens–Bansko route is one of the most popular cross-border journeys in the Balkans for good reason. Thousands of British expats based in Greece, Greek property owners with chalets in Bansko, and international visitors using Athens as a hub all face the same question: what's the best way to get there?

I've personally made this journey dozens of times — both in the construction phase of hotel projects and with clients. This guide gives you an honest comparison of every option available, without the usual affiliate bias.

The Route: What You Need to Know First

The distance from Athens International Airport (ATH) to Bansko is approximately 620km via the E75/E79 route through Thessaloniki and into Bulgaria via Kulata border crossing. The drive takes 6–7 hours in a private vehicle under normal conditions. Border crossing at Kulata–Promachonas (the Greece–Bulgaria crossing) typically adds 20–45 minutes depending on the season.

There is no direct train or affordable flight from Athens to a Bansko-area airport. This means your main options are road-based. The closest Bulgarian airport to Bansko is Sofia (SOF, ~2 hours) or Plovdiv (PDV, ~1.5 hours) — flying Athens to one of these and then taking a private transfer is a viable alternative covered below.

Self-driving gives maximum flexibility but comes with significant caveats. Alternative: Fly Athens → Sofia, Then Private Transfer

For travellers with flexibility on schedule, this is often the most cost-effective luxury option. Olympic Air and Aegean Airlines operate multiple daily flights from Athens to Sofia (approximately 1h10min). Combined with a private transfer from Sofia to Bansko (approximately 2 hours), total journey time is around 4–5 hours including airport time.

  • Athens to Sofia flights: €60–180 one way depending on timing
  • Sofia airport private transfer to Bansko: €120–180 per vehicle
  • Total cost for two people: often comparable to or lower than full door-to-door

The main drawback is the added airport time and the need to manage ski equipment through two airports. For groups of 3–6, the direct private transfer usually wins on simplicity and total cost.

Border Crossing: What to Expect

The main border crossing is Kulata–Promachonas. EU and Schengen passport holders (including UK passports under current bilateral agreements) can cross without a visa. The crossing is generally smooth but can be slow during peak ski season (January weekends). A good private driver will know when to cross and which lane to use.

Key things to have ready:

  • Valid passport (not just ID card — Bulgaria is not in Schengen)
  • Vehicle insurance valid for Bulgaria (your private transfer company handles this)
  • If renting: confirm your rental agreement explicitly permits Bulgaria entry
